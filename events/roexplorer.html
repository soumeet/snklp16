The winner isn't the one with the fastest bot, it's the one who refuses to lose..
Will it be the same with you?? Show your guts and adrenaline at sankalp!

It's time to place your robot in the challenging arena comprising of all possible terrains like sand, pebbles, obstacles, bumpers, bridges, marbles, inclines, sea-saw, dips, cliffs, ramps, banks, etc.

Robot Specifications:

1. The maximum dimensions of the robot should be 25x25x25 cm (Â± 5% allowance)
2. The bots can be powered on-board as well as externally
3. In case of external power supply, the wires must be kept long enough to cover the whole track and wire should remain slack during the complete run
4. Maximum weight of the bot must not exceed 4kgs (Maximum)
5. The power supply must not exceed 18V DC (Maximum)

Arena Specifications:

1. Track width is 40 cm normally and it may change at some places
2. The track surface and course line may have unevenness
3. There will be an inclination, but this will not exceed 45 degrees
4. The arena will consist of various obstacles along with sand, bumps, water and other multi-terrain features

General Rules:

1. This is a team event. The maximum number of participants allowed in a team is 4
2. A team may consist of students from different colleges/institutes
3. The bot must be controlled manually
4. All students must have their respective college/institute ID cards
5. A participant cannot be a member of 2 different teams in this event
6. Any action violating fair play will lead to immediate disqualification
7. It should be noted that the main chassis should remain the same throughout the event
8. The participants will be provided with 220 volts, 50 Hz standard AC supply
9. Scoring rules will be briefed by co-ordinators on spot
Note: All the above rules and specifications may change without prior notice. Decision of event Co-ordinators will be final and binding
